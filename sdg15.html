Goal 15: Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss.

SDG 15 aims to protect and restore the worldâ€™s forests, combat desertification, and halt the loss of biodiversity. Sustainable management of natural resources and ecosystem preservation are critical for ensuring sustainable development and the well-being of future generations.

The goal emphasizes the importance of biodiversity, sustainable forest management, and the restoration of degraded ecosystems to achieve a balanced relationship between humans and nature.

Key Targets:

  • Ensure the conservation of mountain ecosystems, including their biodiversity.
  • Combat desertification, restore degraded land and soil, and strive to achieve a land degradation-neutral world.
  • Protect and prevent the extinction of threatened species.

Why It Matters:

Healthy ecosystems are vital for human survival and well-being. They provide essential services, such as clean air and water, climate regulation, and fertile soil for agriculture. Protecting biodiversity ensures that we can sustain these services for generations to come.
