Goal 6: Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.

SDG 6 aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030. Clean water and sanitation are essential to the health of individuals and communities, as well as the overall economic development.

This goal emphasizes the need for access to safe and affordable drinking water, adequate and equitable sanitation, and hygiene facilities for all. It also focuses on improving water quality, protecting water-related ecosystems, and increasing water-use efficiency to ensure sustainability.

Key Targets:

  • Achieve universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water.
  • Ensure access to adequate and equitable sanitation and hygiene for all.
  • Improve water quality by reducing pollution and minimizing the release of hazardous chemicals.
  • Protect and restore water-related ecosystems, such as rivers, wetlands, and lakes.

Why It Matters:

Water is a precious resource that is vital for human survival and well-being. By ensuring access to clean water and sanitation, we can reduce waterborne diseases, improve health outcomes, and foster economic growth.
