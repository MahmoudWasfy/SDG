Goal 1: End poverty in all its forms everywhere.

SDG 1 aims to eradicate extreme poverty by 2030, currently defined as people living on less than $1.90 a day. This goal also focuses on reducing the proportion of men, women, and children of all ages living in poverty according to national definitions. Tackling poverty is essential as it is a fundamental cause of many other global issues, such as hunger, health problems, and lack of education. Achieving SDG 1 requires actions such as creating social protection systems, improving access to essential services, and promoting sustainable livelihoods to ensure that the most vulnerable populations can escape the cycle of poverty.

Key Targets:

  • End extreme poverty for all people everywhere.
  • Reduce by half the number of people living in poverty in all its dimensions.
  • Implement nationally appropriate social protection systems and measures for all.
  • Ensure equal access to resources and basic services, including education and health care.

Why It Matters:

Poverty is not just about lack of income; it encompasses lack of access to education, healthcare, and opportunities. By ending poverty, we can create a foundation for economic growth, social stability, and sustainable development.
