Goal 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.

SDG 11 aims to ensure access for all to adequate, safe, and affordable housing and basic services, and to upgrade slum settlements. The goal also promotes sustainable urbanization and seeks to provide access to safe, affordable, and sustainable transport systems.

Rapid urbanization poses challenges, including pollution, inadequate infrastructure, and social inequities. Addressing these challenges is crucial for the sustainability of cities and communities worldwide.

Key Targets:

  • Ensure access to safe and affordable housing for all.
  • Provide access to safe, affordable, and sustainable transport systems.
  • Reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities.

Why It Matters:

Sustainable urban development is vital for economic growth and development. Cities are centers of economic activity, and their growth should be managed to ensure that all citizens benefit from urbanization.
